UN-TILTED
A Ryzo Afterschool Activation
Film: Release the Beast  ·  Game: Rage Fighters  ·  Character: Sticky Stan
Event Narrative
The journey of a young gamer through high-stakes psychological territory into emotional self-mastery.
Before You Begin — Facilitator Only
"Today we're going to move through three worlds — animation, art, and gaming. They're all connected by one thing: the way emotions show up in the stories we love and the games we play. By the end, you'll have something on the wall that's yours."
Say this before opening the deck. No slide needed — just you and the room. Keep it loose. Then launch Slide 1.

Part One
The Creative Hook
Recognition — getting kids to see themselves

The Animation Insight
SAY
"The heart of a great animated character is its ability to show EMOTION. Artists work hard on 'expression sheets' before they ever start animating — to understand how a face moves when they feel things."

Meet Sticky Stan
SAY
"This is Sticky Stan — an expression test who comes to life when his creator gets in trouble. Look at these expressions. Pick one that looks like something you've FELT. Draw the version of that feeling on your sticky note. Write the emotion on the back."
SHOW
Sticky Stan angry emotion slides.
SHARE
Show your sticky note. Share the emotion it's representing.

Part Two
The Cinematic Deep Dive
Empathy — feeling it through Aaron's story
Slide
6
The Film — Release the Beast
SAY
"We're going to watch a film about a kid named Aaron. Try to pay attention to the range of emotions you see the characters experience."
SHOW

Slide
7
What's Underneath?
ASK
"Did you see the emotion you drew in the film? What other emotions did Aaron experience?"
SAY
"Anger never comes alone. It's always protecting something else — hurt, embarrassment, frustration. Look at your first sticky. Think of the moment just before that feeling hit. Was it fear? Confusion? Draw that 'underneath' feeling on your SECOND sticky note."
SHOW
"Underneath" expression gallery. Hide the second note under the first.
SHARE
Show your new sticky note. Share the emotion it's representing.
3
Part Three
The Training Ground
Application — players enter Rage Fighters
Slide
8
Entering Rage Fighters
SAY
"Learning how to make good choices even when you're dealing with big emotions is a real skill. Now we enter the game Aaron built: Rage Fighters. You can fight your way through the lands — but your primary mission today is to find the Crumpled Paper Easter Eggs. These are Aaron's notebook pages. They contain Intel on the enemies and the Skills you need to beat them."
DO
Launch Roblox. Display a screenshot of a "Crumpled Paper" egg.
Slide
9
The Skill Tree Dashboard
SAY
"As you play, keep these four skills in mind. Find them in the game to unlock your power."
SHOW
PAUSE
Teleport + Forcefield — Logic catching up to Emotion
BREATHE
Reset your body's panic response — Biology hack
COOL DOWN
Slow the chaos — Literal physical reset
CONNECT
Call for backup — Some bosses can't be soloed
4
Part Four
The Debrief
Ownership — kids claim their skill
Slide
10
The Final Intel Connect the game to the drawing
SAY
"Time's up — head back to your seats. In the game, you found Aaron's intel to beat the Tilt Shogun and the Mind Ninjas. Now look at your original Yellow Sticky Note — the one with your 'Beast' on it."
ASK
"Now that you've played the game, which one skill would have helped you most in the moment you drew that face? Was it a Pause to get distance? A Breathe to reset? Or did you need to Connect with someone?"
DO
Have them write the name of that skill on a THIRD sticky note in big letters.
Slide
11
The #StickyReveal
SAY
"Sometimes a big emotion feels like a mask. We're going to do a #StickyReveal."
DO
Photo 1 — The Mask: Hold Yellow Sticky over face.  Photo 2 — The Reveal: Drop sticky to show the 'Builder' underneath.
Slide
12
The Social Mural
SAY
"Add both stickies to our community wall. You are the builder — and you have the skills to stay Un-Tilted."
DO
Make a mural on your community wall with all the stickies. Then take a photo of the full mural and post it with #StickyReveal #RyzoStudios — tag us so we can share your wall.
